# Commercial License — Placeholder

**This is a placeholder document. It is not a final legal agreement.**

## Purpose

This placeholder documents the intended scope of a future manageCallAI commercial
license. It exists to:

1. Clarify what a commercial agreement would cover.
2. Align the product roadmap with commercial packaging goals.
3. Provide maintainers with a starting point for legal review.

---

## Intended future scope of commercial agreements

The following topics are expected to be addressed in a future commercial license.
**None of these terms are binding today.**

### Pro edition commercial terms (planned)

- License grant for Pro-tier features and modules.
- Permitted use: internal business use for the licensee.
- Restrictions on redistribution and resale.
- Per-seat or per-tenant pricing model (TBD).
- Term: annual subscription (TBD).

### Enterprise edition commercial terms (planned)

- License grant for Enterprise-tier features and modules.
- Permitted use: internal business use and managed deployments (with separate MSP terms).
- Volume licensing and multi-tenant deployment rights.
- Priority support SLA (response time, availability, escalation — TBD).
- Private module distribution rights.
- Audit rights for the licensor.
- Term: annual or multi-year subscription (TBD).

### Hosted / SaaS commercial terms (planned)

- License grant for hosting manageCallAI as a managed service.
- Monthly or annual subscription based on tenant count or call volume (TBD).
- Uptime SLA and support tier (TBD).
- Data processing agreement (separate document, required for GDPR).

### Managed service provider / reseller terms (planned)

- Reseller rights: permitted to offer manageCallAI to end customers.
- Redistribution restrictions: cannot relicense the software.
- Revenue share or wholesale pricing model (TBD).
- Written agreement required; no implied reseller rights.

---

## What this document does NOT cover

- Final pricing or payment terms.
- Tax, VAT, or invoicing obligations.
- Data processing or privacy obligations (separate DPA required).
- SLA commitments (requires separate SLA schedule).
- IP ownership disputes.
- Governing law or jurisdiction (requires legal input).
